Indexing HTTPS pages by default

"...we're adjusting our indexing system to look for more HTTPS pages."

"...weâ€™ll start crawling HTTPS equivalents of HTTP pages, even when the former are not linked to from any page. "

Why is Google doing this?

  • Promote a more secure web
  • Reduce risk to users
  • Prevent eves-dropping & man-in-the-middle attacks

What will change?

  • HTTPS pages will be ranked higher then HTTP
  • Google will crawl HTTPS versions of HTTP pages
  • Google may not index your HTTPS pages

Why HTTPS page may not be indexed

  • Insecure dependencies
  • Redirects to HTTP pages
  • Invalid TLS certificate

What should you do?

  • Audit existing non-secure sites
  • Talk with clients about security
  • Include security in all estimates
